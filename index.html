My Top 10 Favorite Pokémon

A personal list of my favorites!

A personal website created by Brett Craig

1. Sableye

View Wiki Page

Sableye has been my all-time favorite for a while. While it started as a casual appreciation for the odd gremlin Pokemon due to its usefulness in one of my first attempts at a Nuzlocke challenge, I grew more fond of it as time went on with it at the top of my list. What started as a simple appreciation for the design and personality grew the more I used it on my various teams and explored its strengths and weaknesses. I love it’s inspired, unique design, and being one of the few Pokemon in it’s conception to have 0 type weaknesses gives it an element of specialty that not many other Pokemon have. While there are many Pokemon whose designs, usefulness, and general appeal at this point in my life might eclipse Sableye, due to how long it’s remained my favorite, I don’t see it’s placement changing anytime soon.

2. Toxtricity

View Wiki Page

Toxtricity is a recent favorite of mine, but it’s quickly shot up in my list in said recent times. First using it through a gifted egg in my first playthrough of Pokemon Shield, I was immediately struck by the genius of its design. Taking the idea of a poisonous amphibian and turning it into a rock musician is an idea I couldn’t even fathom coming up with myself, yet the design works flawlessly. That’s not even to mention it’s strong, flexible typing of Electric/Poison, it’s different forms to collect, and a whole Gigantamax form to use in battle. There’s a lot to love about Toxtricity, and the personality it exudes straight from that stellar design drew it to me instantly.

3. Mismagius

View Wiki Page

Mismagius is another Pokemon that I quickly was drawn to due to its great design. Being one of the many Pokemon to receive a surprise evolution with the release of Pokemon Diamond and Pearl, I never thought much of it’s pre-evolution, Misdreavus, besides the vague inspiration from Japanese myth. However, with the release of its newfound evolution, Mismagius quickly caught my attention more. A witch-ghost hybrid is a great idea for a design, and sporting powerful moves early on like Shadow Ball and Mystical Fire make it a great offensive choice for any team. Additionally, it makes a strong impression within its release game, being the heavy-hitting ace for the gym leader Fantina. While not super commonly available within most games, Mismagius remains one of my favorite designs for a Pokemon, and that combined with its usefulness ensures a high spot on my favorite’s list.

4. Seismatoad

View Wiki Page

Seismitoad is an unusual pick for me. It’s a Pokemon that I think most people would agree is fairly “ugly” in its design, which generally makes it unappealing to the average person. However, I find there to be something oddly charming about it. The generally unappealing design makes it pretty unique, and stands out among a lot of other choices. Although, the main appeal for Seismitoad for me lies within its usefulness. Like its typing predecessor, Swampert, Water/Ground is an amazing type combination, making use of strong special attacking prowess and heavy physical bulk in order to take hits and give them back in return. And its unique abilities, Poison Point and Dry Skin, make it adaptable to a lot of different scenarios in battle. Though it’s an unusual pick, Seismitoad has grown on me after using it plenty of times as a heavy-hitting team member.

5. Buizel

View Wiki Page

Buizel has been a long-standing favorite of mine. Beyond its appealing design, utilizing flotation devices with a standard sea otter design with good color use, this is one Pokemon that made a big impression on me after seeing it in the anime. The Sinnoh anime in particular was one I watched over and over, due to having a box set of a handful of episodes from it in my possession when I was younger. Due to this, I would quite often see the charming, battle-friendly personality it exuded within the anime, and would inspire me to have it near the top of my list for a long time. Though other Pokemon have eclipsed it in my personal rankings over the years, it being a favorite of mine when I was younger keeps it in the top half of my list.

6. Haunter

View Wiki Page

Haunter is a basic but cool favorite of mine. Its other forms throughout its evolution line, Gastly and Gengar, have their own merits, but fall flat in my eyes due to a general lack of an appealing design. Haunter doesn’t share these problems, its unique shape language and separate floating hands giving it more personality than the rounded, more bland shapes of its other forms. In addition to this, unlike Gengar, which requires a trade with another copy or another person to obtain in game, Haunter is much easier to acquire and use, gaining points in that category for me. Combine that with the general playful personality it shows off in other mediums like the anime, and it’s not a hard choice to put Haunter on this list.

7. Zangoose

View Wiki Page

Zangoose is another favorite carried mostly for its design. Simply speaking, the design of Zangoose is the definition of “cool”. Its serious expression, pronounced claws, the almost edgy-designed red accents of its fur, all make it a super appealing design to me. In addition, it gains points for uniqueness due to not only not having an evolution, but having an in-lore rivalry with another Pokemon, Seviper. While not a super popular choice, in terms of favorites or general usability, Zangoose stands out as a particular favorite of mine due to just how cool it is.

8. Lopunny

View Wiki Page

It’s not what you think! Internet infamy aside, I really like Lopunny as a Pokemon. I mainly gained a high appreciation for the Normal-type after using it in one of my teams during my playthrough of the Pokemon Diamond remake, and utilizing its unique movepool to effectively and strategically take down the champion fight. Having access to the high power move Return, while also sporting a high friendship level naturally due to its unique evolution method, makes it a strong, quick member on a given team. Combine that with an added Mega Evolution down the line, and Lopunny stands out as a flexible, useful team member, and one of my favorites.

9. Sandy Shocks

View Wiki Page

When first playing through Pokemon Scarlet, I knew throughout the incredibly unique designs of the Paradox Pokemon, that one of them would be making it onto my favorites list. And though there were a few strong contenders, none of them made me smile upon discovering it as much as Sandy Shocks. A primitive version of the Pokemon Magneton, Sandy Shocks stands out in design with the added element of iron filings sticking to its extended magnetic limbs, such a simple change giving it loads of personality. It goes beyond just the “Existing Pokemon but Primitive” designs of most of the Paradox Pokemon found within Pokemon Scarlet, but builds on that idea with a realistic design idea that could easily pass off as its own branching evolution for Magneton. Combine that with its unique Electric/Ground type, and it comes off as really unique and worthy of its spot.

10. Pidgeotto

View Wiki Page

Finally, rounding out the bottom of my list is Pidgeotto. It may seem a little boring, sure, and in a lot of senses it is, but I always thought Pidgeotto was the coolest out of the line of 3 birds. I’ve always gravitated towards the middle evolutions of a given line, generally seeing their designs as “cooler” than the general “cute” and “powerful/majestic” designs of the first and final forms respectively. Pidgeotto is a perfect example of this, sporting unique design elements that set it apart from the rest of the line. All that combined with how easily accessible it is in almost any Pokemon game, it’s grown on me enough to make it right at the bottom of my top 10 list.
